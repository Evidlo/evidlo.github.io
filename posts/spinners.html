Can Spinners

2024-01-05

I came up with these little spinning doodads a few years ago around Christmas and I haven't seen anything like them online, so I'm writing about them here as they are quite fun to make. I've just been calling these things "can spinners" (ChatGPT suggested "twirluminum").

They are cut from aluminum soda cans and spin on sharpened tips of wire from the heat rising off the lamp.

I got my family to create their own this year and it was interesting to see the variety of designs they came up with.

Construction

All the spinners shown above are made from sides and bottoms of recycled soda cans.

My process usually goes like this:

  1. Cut out blanks

  2. Create the central dimple

I usually just use a hard, blunt object like a ballpoint pen to push a dimple into the aluminum without crushing it. Can bottoms are made from thicker aluminum, so I had to gently tap on the back of the pen with a hammer to make the dimple.

  1. Cut out blades and bend into shape.

  2. Trim material as necessary for balance.
